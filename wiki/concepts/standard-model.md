# Standard Model

## Definition

The Standard Model of particle physics describes elementary particles and their interactions (except gravity). It contains: 6 quarks, 6 leptons, 4 gauge bosons (photon, W, Z, gluons), and the Higgs boson. Gauge group: SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) / Z₆.

## Why It Matters

The Standard Model works with astonishing precision — predictions match experiments to many decimal places. Yet it has ~19 free parameters (masses, mixing angles, coupling constants) that must be measured. Why this particular group? Why these masses? Why three generations?

## How It Works
