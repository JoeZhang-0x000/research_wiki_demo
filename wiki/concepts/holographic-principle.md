# Holographic Principle

## Definition

The holographic principle is the conjecture that all information contained in a region of space can be represented by a theory on the boundary of that region. The maximum entropy scales with area, not volume — a sphere of radius R can encode at most ~A/4 bits where A is its surface area.

## Why It Matters

The holographic principle resolves apparent paradoxes in black hole thermodynamics and provides a framework for quantum gravity. It suggests spacetime is not fundamental but emerges from a lower-dimensional quantum system. Different implementations (AdS/CFT, OPH) apply this principle in different ways.

## How It Works

### In AdS/CFT
A conformal field theory (CFT) on an n-dimensional boundary encodes physics of Anti-de Sitter space in n+1 dimensions. Correlation functions in the CFT compute bulk scattering amplitudes.

### In OPH (Observer Patch Holography)
A finite-capacity S² holographic screen encodes physics for observers whose patches overlap. Consistency across patch overlaps forces specific physics (Lorentz symmetry, gauge structure, particle spectrum). The screen's conformal group SO⁺(3,1) directly produces 3+1D Lorentzian kinematics.

## Key Properties / Tradeoffs

- **Information bound**: entropy scales as area, not volume
- **Boundary vs bulk**: physics in the bulk emerges from boundary degrees of freedom
- **Different implementations**: AdS/CFT uses geometric boundary; OPH uses observer-overlap consistency
