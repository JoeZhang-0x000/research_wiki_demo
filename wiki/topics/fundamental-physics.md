# Fundamental Physics

## Scope

Fundamental physics covers the deepest questions about the nature of reality: what is spacetime, how does gravity emerge, what is the origin of the Standard Model particle spectrum, and how do quantum mechanics and gravity unify. This topic spans quantum gravity, gauge theory, cosmology, and the measurement problem.

## Subproblems

1. **Quantum Gravity** — unifying quantum mechanics with general relativity
2. **Spacetime Origin** — why 3+1D Lorentzian spacetime rather than other dimensions
3. **Gauge Symmetry** — origin of SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) structure
4. **Particle Mass Hierarchy** — why particles have the masses they do
5. **Measurement Problem** — why quantum measurement yields definite outcomes
6. **Cosmological Constant** — why Lambda has the value it does

## Key Approaches

### Approach 1: String Theory
Attempts to replace point particles with one-dimensional strings, naturally incorporating gravity and unifying gauge interactions. However, produces vast landscape of possible universes.

### Approach 2: Loop Quantum Gravity
Quantizes spacetime directly using spin networks and spin foams. Makes progress on black hole entropy but struggles to recover low-energy physics like the Standard Model.

### Approach 3: Holographic Principles
Proposes that physics in a volume is encoded on its boundary. AdS/CFT is the most developed example. Observer Patch Holography (OPH) applies holographic ideas differently — from observer overlaps rather than geometric boundaries.

### Approach 4: Observer-Synchronization (OPH)
Physics emerges from consistency requirements across overlapping observer patches on a holographic screen. Derives 3+1D spacetime, Einstein gravity, and Standard Model from first principles.
