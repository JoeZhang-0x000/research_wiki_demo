# Gauge Theory

## Definition

A gauge theory is a field theory where the Lagrangian is invariant under continuous symmetry transformations (gauge symmetry), but the physical fields are not uniquely determined — different "gauge choices" describe the same physical situation. The Standard Model is built from SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) gauge symmetry.

## Why It Matters

Gauge symmetry determines how particles interact. Electromagnetism arises from U(1), the weak force from SU(2), and strong force from SU(3). The Standard Model gauge group — why exactly SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) / Z₆ — has historically been assumed rather than derived.
