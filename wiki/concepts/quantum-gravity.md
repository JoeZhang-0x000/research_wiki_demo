# Quantum Gravity

## Definition

Quantum gravity is the field attempting to unify quantum mechanics with general relativity. Quantum mechanics describes physics at small scales; general relativity describes gravity and the structure of spacetime. They are fundamentally incompatible at high energies or small scales.

## Why It Matters

Without quantum gravity, we cannot answer what happens at singularities (black holes, Big Bang), understand the fundamental nature of spacetime, or unify all forces. Every attempt (string theory, loop quantum gravity, OPH) takes a different approach.

## How It Works

### Traditional Approaches
- **String theory**: replaces point particles with strings; gravity emerges naturally but requires extra dimensions and produces vast landscape
- **Loop quantum gravity**: quantizes spacetime directly via spin networks; progress on black hole entropy but hard to recover Standard Model

### OPH Approach
Gravity is what large-scale consistency looks like when local patch algebras, modular flow, generalized entropy, and first-law structure coexist. A Jacobson-style small-ball argument: thermodynamics on holographic screens forces Einstein equations at large scales. The graviton emerges as a structural zero from overlap consistency.

## Key Properties / Tradeoffs

- **Massless graviton**: OPH derives it as structural output, not postulate
- **Black hole entropy**: OPH provides structural frame but derivations still in progress
- **Experimental tests**: OPH has IBM quantum hardware benchmarks
